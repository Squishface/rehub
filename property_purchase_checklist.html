▼ 🏦 CREATIVE FINANCING FOR ACQUISITIONS
Key Principle: Creative financing is the way around traditional lending binds. Select which method(s) you're using for this property to help you get in with less of your own money and lower monthly debt service. Master these 6 options.
The seller carries the mortgage for you. You make payments to seller instead of bank. Requires seller to agree and lender approval (if property has existing mortgage). Advantage: More flexible terms, lower down payment possible, faster closing.
You take responsibility for the seller's existing mortgage (when allowed by lender). Advantage: Keep seller's original loan terms (often better than current rates), lower interest rate than new loan. Requirement: Lender must approve assumption and your creditworthiness.
Owner offers you a new loan while keeping and paying down their original loan (the new loan "wraps" the original). You pay owner, owner pays original lender. Advantage: Seller benefits from rate difference (incentivizes them), you get creative terms and lower down payment. Risk: Seller must stay current on original loan.
Lease the property from seller until you have the equity or cash to buy it. Option gives you right (not obligation) to purchase at set price. Advantage: Build equity through rent credits, lock in price now, test property before full ownership. Rent payments go toward purchase price if exercised.
You obtain a second loan to cover your down payment on a primary mortgage loan (primary mortgage lender doesn't know about second). Advantage: Reduce your out-of-pocket down payment significantly. Note: Secondary liens are generally secured against the property and first in line if deal goes sour.
You involve other investors and partners in your acquisitions. Pool capital together to make deal happen. Advantage: Access more cash without personal liability, share risk among partners. Tradeoff: Complex legal structure, need to negotiate terms with partners, reduced control and profit share.
Borrowing Stages: Your ability to borrow goes through three stages: Credit (creditworthiness from credit score, payment history), Equity (having savings/assets available as down payment), and Cash Flow (property income to cover loan payments). Lenders evaluate all three.

Secondary Liens: Private seconds and wraps carry less favorable terms than primary mortgages. First liens are generally secured against the property. Know that secondary lenders account for this in their pricing.

Syndication Strategy: Any of these 6 financing scenarios can be syndicated if you are unable or unwilling to provide cash or collateral. Negotiate the deal and bring in partners and investors. Requires clear legal agreements on roles, capital contributions, profit sharing, and exit strategy.
▼ 💰 FINANCIAL MODEL: TWO WEALTH ENGINES
Key Principle: There are two ways to build wealth in real estate: Equity Buildup (price appreciation + debt paydown) and Cash Flow Growth (monthly income after expenses).
Understand the Path of Money
Money has a path and you must guide it to places that yield greatest financial growth and most substantial net worth.
Create a Budget for Investments
Stay on the Path. Differentiate between what you want and what you need. Make personal discipline to stick to it.
Check Your Personal Net Worth
Keep household P&L and balance sheet to track net worth over time. Examine total often and ask: "How can I make it grow?"
Equity Buildup Strategy
Increases your net worth in real estate assets. Comes from twin forces of price appreciation and debt paydown.
Cash Flow Growth Strategy
Provides stream of unearned income. Possible when you buy right, finance wisely and control your expenses.
Master the "Buy It Right—Pay It Down—Pay It Off" Motto
Buy Right: make your money going in. Pay It Down: add significantly to Equity Buildup. Pay It Off: ensure growing stream of unearned income where money works for you.
▼ 🎯 FIVE LAWS OF LEAD GENERATION
Key Principle: A strong Lead Generation Model removes luck from the equation. Quality is in the quantity—you must look at many deals to find the right one.
Law #1: Never Compromise
Bear in mind your Strict Criteria and never violate it. Strong desire to do deal can lead to trouble.
Law #2: Be a Shopper, Not a Buyer
Better to miss a good deal than to buy a bad one. Love the process, the thrill of the hunt.
Law #3: Timing Matters
Be first or last to make an offer. Look for advantage of an uncompetitive environment.
Law #4: It's a Numbers Game
The more suspects you investigate, the better your sense of true prospects. Investigate 50 deals for every 1 you buy.
Law #5: Be Organized and Systematic
Building great criteria requires focus over time. Refine criteria continually to understand your market.
▼ 🏗️ ACQUISITION MODEL: TWO STRATEGIES
Key Principle: By following the Acquisition Model and buying right, you virtually guarantee success. The key? Make your money going in.
Strategy #1: Buy for Cash
Use one of four strategies: Find & Refer; Control & Assign; Buy & Sell; Buy, Improve & Sell (BRRRR).
Strategy #2: Buy for Cash Flow and Equity
Explore three options: Lease Option; Buy & Hold; Buy, Improve & Hold.
Master Buy & Sell Strategy
Looking for: cash, ensuring fast net profit payoff. MUST know your numbers going in. Use Terms Worksheet: Buy & Sell.
Master Buy & Hold Strategy
The TRUE wealth-building option. Long-term using twin forces of Equity Buildup and Cash Flow Growth. Use Terms Worksheet: Buy & Hold.
▼ 🤝 WORK NETWORK MODEL: THREE CIRCLES
Key Principle: Your Work Network is your investing lifeline. Build it by bringing together a powerful group of people.
Inner Circle: Monthly Touchpoints
Select group you trust most. Mentors, consultants, partners, informal board of directors. Meet monthly.
Support Circle: Per Transaction
Key fiduciary people in your investment life. Professionals you rely on for transaction details and needed connections.
Service Circle: As Needed
Specialized independent contractors and freelancers who perform specific functions for a particular property.
Work Your Work Network
Build it, maintain it, engage it. Finding right people takes time. Build systematically, maintain relationships, engage professionally.
▼ 📜 FIVE RULES OF ENGAGEMENT
Key Principle: How you conduct yourself determines your long-term success and reputation in real estate investing.
Rule #1: Do Deals
Action and execution are essential. Actually do it.
Rule #2: Keep Your Word
Your reputation is everything. Follow through on commitments without exception.
Rule #3: Don't Talk Badly About Anyone
No one trusts a gossip. Keep negative comments to yourself. Maintain professional courtesy always.
Rule #4: Don't Shortchange Anyone
Deal fairly. Honor commitments. Pay what you promised. Don't nickel-and-dime people.
Rule #5: Refer Business
Share opportunities. Pass leads to your network. Build goodwill through generosity and reciprocity.
▼ 💼 ISSUE 13: TAX MINIMIZATION & 1031 EXCHANGES
Key Principle: Minimize tax exposure through detailed accounting, strategic depreciation, and leveraging 1031 exchanges to defer capital gains taxes. Work with a qualified CPA specializing in real estate.
Hire Qualified CPA Early
Engage a CPA specializing in real estate investment BEFORE acquisition. Examples: John Haughney CPA. This person is a key member of your Work Network.
Track All Expenses Comprehensively
Document all acquisition costs: lead generation, inspections, finder's fees, real estate commissions, property evaluations. Keep detailed records during ownership for tax deductions.
Depreciate the Property
Use depreciation to offset rental income. The government requires property depreciation. Consult CPA on depreciation schedules and recapture rules.
Deduct Operating Expenses
Property repairs, improvements, maintenance, and expenses adding value are deductible. Use to offset rental income. Can be recaptured for years after incurred.
Understand 1031 Exchange Rules
A 1031 exchange allows deferral of capital gains taxes when selling one property and buying another (replacement property). NOT a property swap—you sell and reinvest proceeds into a new property to defer taxes.
Timeline: 45 Days + 180 Days
After selling a "relinquished property," you have 45 days to identify a replacement property. You have a total of 180 days to close. Begin searching for replacement property before or during marketing of your sale.
Use a Qualified Intermediary
Required: A qualified intermediary must facilitate the 1031 exchange. They hold the sale proceeds and purchase the replacement property. Consult your CPA to identify and vet qualified intermediaries.
1031 Exchange vs. Direct Property Swap
Many believe 1031 is swapping properties directly. FALSE. A 1031 exchange is designed to SELL one property and REPLACE it with another, deferring capital gains taxes. Direct swaps are possible but NOT the 1031 mechanism.
Defer Capital Gains Indefinitely
Repeat 1031 exchanges across multiple properties to defer capital gains taxes indefinitely. Keep purchasing larger, more expensive properties. Pay taxes only when you stop exchanging and hold final property.
Consider IRAs & Qualified Accounts
Use IRAs and 1031 exchanges together with CPA guidance for additional tax deferral strategies. Complex planning—requires qualified tax professional oversight.
Engage CPA Before Sale
Plan 1031 exchanges BEFORE closing a sale. Your CPA and qualified intermediary must coordinate. Late planning can disqualify the exchange or incur penalties.
