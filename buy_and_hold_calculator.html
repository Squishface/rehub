📧 How to Communicate Rent Increases to Tenants
1. Know Your State's Laws
Most states require 30-60 days written notice. Check your state's tenant laws—some have rent increase caps or specific procedures.

2. Professional Written Notice (Best Practice)
Send a formal letter 60 days before the increase takes effect. Include:
• Current rent amount
• New rent amount
• Effective date
• Reason (optional but helpful: "market-rate adjustment to reflect current property value")
• How to pay the new amount

3. What To Say (Keep It Professional)
"Dear [Tenant], As of [date], your monthly rent will increase from $2,000 to $2,060 per month. This 3% increase reflects the current rental market for properties in this area. Thank you for being a valued tenant."

4. Timing Tips
• Schedule increases at lease renewal time if possible
• Stay at/below market rate to keep good tenants
• Build annual increases into leases (e.g., "rent increases 3% annually on renewal")

5. Relationship Management
• Good tenants are valuable—increases should be gradual (2-4% typical)
• If tenant is unreliable, you might NOT increase rent
• Document lease terms so no one is surprised

❌ What NOT To Do
• Don't raise rent after a tenant files a complaint (illegal retaliation)
• Don't raise excessively—risk tenant leaving
• Don't give less notice than your state requires
• Don't surprise tenants—this causes turnover
